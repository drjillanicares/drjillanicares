About Me

Your Trusted Physiotherapist

Dr. Aqib Raza Jillani PT
Doctor of Physical Therapy · DPT
✅ AHPC 🏛️ PPTA 🇺🇸 APTA 🌍 WHO/UN 🇬🇧 OpenLearn

I'm Dr. Aqib Raza Jillani PT — an AHPC Registered Physiotherapist with a Doctor of Physical Therapy (DPT) degree, recognized by the Higher Education Commission (HEC) of Pakistan. I offer in-clinic and telehealth consultations nationwide and internationally — contact me directly via phone or WhatsApp to book.

With 5.6+ years of progressive clinical experience across leading institutions including Shaikh Zayed Hospital, Lahore, DHQ Teaching Hospital, Sargodha, and City Physical Therapy, Islamabad, I specialize in orthopedic, neurological, musculoskeletal, sports, paediatric, and cardiopulmonary rehabilitation — always guided by evidence-based, patient-centered principles.

My international certifications include the American Physical Therapy Association (APTA), World Health Organization (WHO), the United Nations Agora Platform, and The Open University UK — reflecting my commitment to globally informed, continuously updated clinical practice.

"Health is Wealth — and I'm here to protect yours. Let me explain your condition, your treatment, and your recovery — clearly, completely, and with care."

Follow along as @drjillanicares on all social platforms for free physiotherapy education in Urdu and English every week. Whether you visit the clinic or connect online, I'm here to help you move better and live better.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a doctor's referral to see a physiotherapist?+
No, you do not need a referral in most cases. You can directly book an appointment with Dr. Aqib via WhatsApp or by visiting the clinic. However, if you have recent X-rays, MRI reports, or doctor notes, please bring them as they help with assessment.
How does a Telehealth / Online consultation work?+
Book via WhatsApp at +92 304 843 8337. A video or call session is arranged at a convenient time. Dr. Aqib conducts a full assessment, discusses your condition, and provides a personalized home exercise program — all online. Available for patients across Pakistan and internationally.
What should I bring to my first clinic appointment?+
Please bring any relevant medical documents such as X-rays, MRI scans, blood reports, previous prescriptions, or doctor's notes. Wear comfortable clothing that allows easy access to the area being treated. Arrive 5–10 minutes early to complete a brief patient form.
How many sessions will I need?+
This depends on your condition, severity, and individual response to treatment. After your initial assessment, Dr. Aqib will give you an estimated treatment timeline and session plan. Acute conditions may resolve in 4–8 sessions, while chronic or neurological conditions may require longer programs.
Is physiotherapy painful?+
Some techniques such as deep tissue massage or joint mobilization may cause mild discomfort, but physiotherapy should never be severely painful. Dr. Aqib always works within your comfort level and adjusts treatment based on your feedback every session.
Can physiotherapy help after surgery?+
Yes — post-surgical rehabilitation is one of the most important aspects of recovery. Physiotherapy helps restore movement, strength, and function after orthopedic surgeries such as knee replacement, ACL repair, spinal surgery, shoulder surgery, and more. Early intervention leads to faster and better outcomes.
Do you treat children?+
Yes. Dr. Aqib provides paediatric physiotherapy for children with developmental delays, cerebral palsy, torticollis, neurological conditions, and more. Sessions are child-friendly and family-involved to ensure the best outcomes.
How do I book an appointment?+
The easiest way is via WhatsApp at +92 304 843 8337. You can also fill out the booking form on this website or send an email to drjillanicares@gmail.com. The clinic address in Sillanwali, Sargodha will be confirmed upon booking (Mon–Fri, 10 AM – 4 PM).
